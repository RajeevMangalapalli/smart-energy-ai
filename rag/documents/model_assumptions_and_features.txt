MODEL ASSUMPTIONS AND FEATURES

This document outlines the engineering approach and underlying assumptions for the energy demand forecasting model based on the German Daily Load dataset.

Engineered Features:
To capture the temporal dynamics of electricity demand, the following features were derived:
- Lag Features (lag_1, lag_7): These capture the autocorrelation in the data. lag_1 represents yesterday's demand, while lag_7 represents the demand from the same day of the previous week, accounting for weekly cycles.
- Rolling Means (rolling_mean_7, rolling_mean_30): These provide a smoothed representation of recent trends, helping the model distinguish between short-term noise and medium-term shifts in consumption levels.

Key Assumptions:
1. Periodicity: The model assumes that historical seasonal patterns (weekly and yearly) will persist into the future.
2. Continuity: It is assumed that there are no sudden, permanent structural shifts in the energy market (e.g., total industrial shutdowns) during the period being forecast.
3. Autocorrelation: The model relies on the assumption that the most recent demand values are the strongest predictors of the immediate future.

Feature Utility:
By incorporating both short-term lags (1-day) and weekly lags (7-days), the model can effectively adjust for both immediate fluctuations and the inherent difference between workdays and weekends.
